Use of cookies and similar technologies

Altrone uses cookies and equivalent technologies, such as pixels to facilitate, protect, improve, and promote our services. For this policy and its purpose, we will pass on all of these forms of technologies as “cookies”. This page constitutes information on what, who, and how these technologies are in association with the Altrone website and Services.

What are cookies?

A cookie is a tiny data file sent to your browser when you go and visit a website. A pixel tag (pixel) is a tiny portion of the system belonging to a third party that can be rooted on the website and in emails. We make use of both, i.e., our own cookies and cookies of a third party. Go and read the section “How does Altrone uses cookies” below for more information on third-party cookies that we use.

 

We also make use of local storage that includes HTML5 local storage and the cache of the browser. Local storage enables us to stock up on information locally on your device or browser to make your experience better. These experiences can be remembering your settings for whether you view your folders as thumbnails or in a list.

How can I manage the data gathered through cookies?

 

Browser settings: You can avoid cookies by setting up your browser. However, this may restrict your use of our services. It is because of the lack of an accepted standard for understanding Do Not Track (DNT) signals our systems don’t answer DNT: 1 signals from browsers that visit our websites. 

 

 Cookie preferences: When you use Altrone’s services, you can prefer which cookies you accept for Altrone to use by selecting Cookies & GDPR choices in the footer of this page under the Support section.
